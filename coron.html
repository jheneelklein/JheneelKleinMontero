Coron, Palawan Island Guide

Your ultimate guide to exploring the stunning natural beauty and rich marine life of Coron, Palawan.

Why Visit Coron?

Coron, part of the Calamian Islands in the northern part of Palawan, Philippines, is known for its crystal-clear lagoons, limestone cliffs, vibrant coral reefs, and historic shipwrecks from WWII. Whether you're an adventurer, nature lover, or beach enthusiast, Coron offers a memorable experience.

Top Attractions in Coron

Kayangan Lake

Known as the cleanest lake in the Philippines, Kayangan Lake is surrounded by towering limestone cliffs and offers a perfect spot for swimming and snorkeling.

Twin Lagoon

A breathtaking pair of lagoons where freshwater meets saltwater, providing a unique swimming experience with stunning rock formations.

Barracuda Lake

Known for its unique thermocline, Barracuda Lake has warm and cool layers of water, ideal for adventurous divers and snorkelers.

Things to Do

Snorkeling

Discover the vibrant coral gardens and marine life, especially in Siete Pecados, one of Coron's most famous snorkeling spots.

Diving

Explore historic WWII shipwrecks and unique underwater landscapes that attract divers from around the world.

Island Hopping

Visit beautiful beaches, secluded islands, and unique rock formations on an island-hopping tour of Coron.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: The dry season (November to May) is ideal for outdoor activities, with clear skies and calm seas.
  • Getting Around: Tricycles are the most common mode of transport in Coron town, and boat tours are essential for reaching the island's main attractions.
  • Entry Fees: Most attractions require a small entrance fee, so keep some cash handy.
  • Environmental Fees: Be mindful of the eco-tourism fees that support the conservation of Coron's natural resources.
